# Airline-Dashboard

Case Study

Objective

To analyze airline ticket pricing, flight distribution, and route patterns for better pricing optimization and operational planning.

Key Insights
1. Flight Distribution by Airline

Indigo has the highest number of flights (~800+), dominating the market.

Air India and SpiceJet follow with moderate flight counts.

GO_FIRST, Vistara, and AirAsia have significantly fewer flights.

2. Stops Analysis

One-stop flights: 54.85%

Non-stop flights: 33.47%

Two or more stops: 11.68%

3. Days Left vs Price

Prices fluctuate heavily as the number of days left decreases.

Last-minute bookings show sharp price spikes (dynamic pricing).

4. Price Variation by Source City

Chennai, Mumbai, and Delhi show higher average prices.

Hyderabad, Bangalore have more stable pricing patterns.

Business Recommendations

Competing airlines should optimize pricing around Indigo-dominated routes.

Implement AI-based dynamic fare prediction models.

Promote early-bird offers to reduce last-minute high-cost bookings.

Source cities with high variance should receive targeted promotional pricing.
